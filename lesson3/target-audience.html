Target Audience

SmithRiverCommerce.com

Location Information

Smith River has pristine emerald waters and abundance of Chinook salmon and Steelhead fishing opportunities. It runs right next to Jedediah Redwoods State park which has some of the largest and tallest trees in the world. It is also a short drive from Crescent City, and the ocean.

Annual number of visitors: approximately 1.5 million

Personas

  • Hardcore Fishermen:

    Rich, retired old men that are looking for guide services to catch Steelhead or Salmon on the Smith River. The influx of these type of visitors generally peaks in early spring, and again in late fall. They typically stay in a local cabin, and spend money at local tackle shops, guide services, restruants, gas stations, bars and liquor stores.

  • Family Travelers:

    Families on vacation come year round to explore the beautiful scenery and park opportunities in the Jedediah Redwoods. In the summer months, many of these type of visitors bring RV's, and pay to stay at local camp sites. Others stay at local hotels, cabins, Ainbnb's and condos. Guided rafting, zipline, and other excursions are typically a part of family travelers plans.

Scenarios

  • Scenario 1:

    A family living in Idaho is planning a trip to visit the Redwoods. They see that the closest Redwood forest is Jedediah Smith, so they start to look for what type of activities the area has to offer. At first glance, the area doesn't appear to have many activities for people other than hiking. Then they come accross our website, which has links to all the unique and interesting local shops, excursions, community events, housing/hotel options, and general information that gets them to decide to come here rather than going to the redwoods in a more developed area. They sign up for a zipline trip, and stay at one of our cabins in the shop-town located at the mouth of the canyon. They enjoy eating at the local restraunts, and the parents go to the bar in the evening while the kids are exploring by the river. One of the days they visit a local farmers market and buy suveneirs.

  • Scenario 2:

    A group of coworkers is trying to plan a Steelhead fishing trip in January. They come across our site which has enticing pictures and information about the great fishing opportunities and guide services that are found here locally. They also see an advertizement for the January Steelhead season that includes a group housing deal at a condo with a discount for scheduling in advance. They link to that website, and come back to the main one to plan out the rest of the events they want to fill their trip with between fishing runs.
